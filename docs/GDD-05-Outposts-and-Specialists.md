# Outposts & Specialists

## Overview

Outposts are Exploration's purposeful infrastructure — forward bases that extend the ministry's reach, anchor the
logistics network, and conduct the analytical and extractive work that survey ships initiate but cannot complete. They
are distinct from colonies, which are Settlement's domain; the player does not manage colonies, but she builds and
directs outposts.

Specialists are the human workforce of the outpost network. They are produced by colonies over time, travel the
logistics network like any other resource, and determine what an outpost is capable of doing well.

## Outposts

Outposts are established by expedition vessels (see Exploration & Survey) and are not typed at founding. The player is
not choosing what kind of outpost to build — she is choosing where to establish a presence. What the outpost becomes is
determined by what she sends there afterward.

Outpost specialization emerges from use. An outpost that receives artifact finds and archaeology specialists develops
analytical depth. One that receives mining equipment and extraction specialists becomes a resource hub. One that
receives both does neither as well as a dedicated facility. The player's primary lever over specialization is routing —
deciding what flows through an outpost is deciding what it becomes.

Deliberate respecialization is possible but costly. Transferring specialists to reshape an outpost's character takes
time and temporarily reduces capability at both the source and destination. Network topology constrains this further: a
remote outpost that is hard to reach may retain whatever character its founding team had, simply because reassignment is
expensive. Respecialization decisions need to be made in anticipation of need, not in response to it.

Outpost viability varies by location. Hostile environments carry higher specialist attrition and greater resource
demand. This is a soft constraint on forward operations — expensive bets are possible, but the cost is real.

## Specialists

Specialists are a personnel resource produced by colonies over time. They are fungible within their type — the game
tracks specialist types as resources, not individuals.

Three broad types are currently defined:

**Archaeology specialists** — conduct artifact excavation, analysis, and outpost characterization work. The primary
workforce of the archaeological operation. Outpost analytical depth in specific artifact dimensions develops from the
combination of archaeology specialists and the artifacts that have flowed through the facility.

**Mining specialists** — operate resource extraction infrastructure. Required to run mining operations at an outpost;
their presence determines extraction throughput.

**Survey specialists** — support extended survey and assessment work from an outpost. Their presence improves the
quality and speed of the intelligence an outpost generates about its home system and surrounding region.

Specialists travel the logistics network like any other resource — they move along established freighter routes, which
means network topology directly constrains organizational flexibility. Getting specialists to a remote outpost requires
an established route and takes time.

A colony going offline affects specialist supply tangibly: fewer specialists flowing into the network means slower
outpost development and harder respecialization decisions downstream.
